About Edunancial

From a single question in the office to a clear path of habits, earning, and responsible investing.

The origin

In the office, just after hanging up a call, my 11-year-old son walked in and asked: “How do I make money with my head?” We didn’t want a lecture. We wanted a plan — small steps, real decisions, and simple tools. That’s how Edunancial began.

The path (ages 11–15)

Age 11 — First Money Habits

  • Saves a small portion of allowance and gifts.
  • Learns delayed gratification.
  • Opens a basic savings account with a parent.

Age 12 — Early Tracking

  • Keeps a simple ledger of money in/out.
  • Uses categories: saving, spending, giving.
  • Understands trade-offs in choices.

Age 13 — Earning & Responsibility

  • Starts small paid jobs for neighbors.
  • Links work → earnings → savings goals.
  • Sets and reaches a target for a big purchase.

Age 14 — Expanding Tools

  • Experiments with budgeting/tracking apps.
  • Explores compound interest with simple examples.
  • Compares “safe” savings vs. higher-risk ideas.

Age 15 — Practical Investing

  • Saves a fixed percentage of every dollar earned.
  • Tracks goals consistently (notebook + app).
  • Earns through small jobs and local projects.
  • Buys physical silver and gold with his own funds.
  • Understands diversification.

This is financial education — not investment advice. Decisions should be made with a responsible adult.

Purpose

Build judgment, responsibility, and confidence so young people can make good decisions with money — now and later. Keep lessons short, tools simple, and progress visible.
