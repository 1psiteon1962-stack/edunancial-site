Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

Last Updated: ____________________

1. Parties

This Data Processing Agreement (“DPA”) forms part of the agreement between Edunancial, Inc. (“Edunancial,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) and any customer or user (“Customer,” “you,” or “your”) who provides personal data to Edunancial in connection with the Services.

2. Roles of the Parties

For the purposes of data protection laws, Edunancial generally acts as a “data controller” for personal data collected through our Site and Services. In limited situations where we process personal data solely on your documented instructions (for example, where you upload contact lists to our systems), we act as a “data processor.”

3. Processing Instructions

When Edunancial acts as a data processor, we will:

  • Process personal data only on your documented instructions;
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data;
  • Ensure confidentiality obligations for personnel with access to personal data;
  • Assist you, where reasonable, in responding to data subject requests;
  • Notify you without undue delay of any personal data breach we become aware of that affects your data.

4. Sub-Processors

You authorize Edunancial to engage third-party service providers (“Sub-Processors”) to support the delivery of the Services. We will ensure that Sub-Processors are bound by written obligations that provide at least the same level of protection for personal data as this DPA.

5. Data Retention and Deletion

Upon termination of the Services or upon your written request, we will delete or return personal data we process on your behalf, unless retention is required by law.

6. Audits

On reasonable notice, and no more than once per year, you may request information reasonably necessary to demonstrate Edunancial’s compliance with this DPA. We may satisfy this obligation by providing certifications, third-party audit reports, or written summaries of our security practices.

7. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the same law and jurisdiction specified in our main Terms of Use.
