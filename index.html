Edunancial was born out of a moment between a father and son. A deposit notification came in from consulting work I’d just completed. My son saw it and asked:

“How did you make money like that?”

I told him, “I worked with my head.” Then I asked, “Do you want to work with your head?” He replied: “Yes, but I don’t know how.”

He was only 11 years old. I didn’t know it then, but that short exchange sparked everything that followed.

From that day on, we began a journey together where my challenge was making everything easy enough for an 11-year-old to understand and to learn step by step while building confidence.

He began immediately. With his allowance, he started buying silver; later gold. Holding assets—not spending on games or candy—taught discipline and the value of building. When his budget allowed, we added stocks, learning ownership through patience and research. He shaped the idea for a dog food company. And while he hasn’t purchased property yet, he studies real estate to be ready.

Our foundation is Red, White, and Blue—real estate, paper assets, and business. People naturally gravitate to one or two; the key is to choose a starting point and begin.

If an 11-year-old could begin the journey, so can you.
