Our Story

One afternoon when my son was 11, he walked in just as an online deposit arrived. His eyes went wide: “How did you make money like that?” I said, “By using my head.” Then I asked, “Do you want to work with your head?” He said, “Yes… but I don’t know how.” That’s when Edunancial was born.

At 11, he started buying silver coins. This taught him discipline and ensured he was putting his money into an asset instead of spending it like many kids do—on games and candy—things that don’t put money back in your pocket.

Around 12 years old, he started making lists of stocks he wanted to purchase. I had him analyze those stocks to see whether they were overvalued or undervalued. After he came back with his answers and reasons, we bought the stocks. His results produced an average ROI of 7–12%.

At 15, he decided to not only purchase silver, but also gold. Today we are exploring other possibilities, including precious gems such as diamonds, rubies, or emeralds.

The Red, White, and Blue methodology isn’t about telling you what to buy. It helps you ask the right question first: Why am I buying this? What is it going to do for me? It puts you in control of your own financial future.

Today, my son continues to use the Red, White, and Blue to build his financial future—he is now planning a dog food business. As a father, I feel good knowing that he has a strong foundation for lifelong learning that will only improve over time.
