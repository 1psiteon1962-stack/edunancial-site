How Edunancial started — and how it grew

The spark: After a client call, my 11-year-old asked, “How do I make money with my head?” That one question became our mission: teach young people how to think about money — clearly, simply, practically.

Age 11 — First Money Habits

  • Saves a small portion of allowance and gifts.
  • Learns delayed gratification (wait vs. impulse).
  • Opens a basic savings account with a parent.

Age 12 — Early Tracking

  • Keeps a simple ledger of money in/out.
  • Uses categories: saving, spending, giving.
  • Understands trade-offs in choices.

Age 13 — Earning & Responsibility

  • Starts small paid jobs for neighbors.
  • Links work → earnings → savings goals.
  • Sets and reaches a target for a big purchase.

Age 14 — Expanding Tools

  • Experiments with budgeting/tracking apps.
  • Compares safe saving vs. higher-risk ideas.
  • Builds judgment through simple examples.

Age 15 — Practical Investing

  • Saves a fixed percentage of every dollar earned.
  • Tracks goals consistently (notebook + app).
  • Buys physical silver and gold with his own funds.
  • Selects stocks with 8–12% ROI; learns diversification.

Educational story — not financial or investment advice.

Learn in stages

  • Basics: Budgeting, saving, goals, and safety.
  • Intermediate: Compound interest, banking, responsibility.
  • Advanced: Diversification, risk awareness, decision analysis.
