Edunancial was born out of a moment between a father and son. One day, as I checked my phone, a deposit notification came in from consulting work I had just completed for a client. My son noticed it and asked:

“How did you make money like that?”

I told him, “I worked with my head.” Then I asked, “Do you want to work with your head?” He replied: “Yes, but I don’t know how.”

He was only 11 years old. I didn’t know it at that moment, but that short exchange became the spark for everything that followed.

From that day on, we began a journey together where my challenge was making everything easy enough for an 11-year-old to understand and to learn step by step while building confidence.

He began immediately. With his allowance, he started buying silver; later, he moved into gold. Holding assets instead of spending on games or candy taught discipline, patience, and the value of building. When his budget allowed, we added stocks—a way to participate in ownership through patience and research. Alongside that, he started shaping the idea for a dog food company. And while he hasn’t purchased property yet, he continues to study real estate to be ready when the time is right.

At Edunancial, our foundation is Red, White, and Blue—not rigid rules, but vehicles that can each lead to wealth:

  • Red — Real Estate: equity and ownership of tangible property.
  • White — Paper Assets: stocks, options, and funds explained in plain terms.
  • Blue — Business: ownership, systems, and scaling to create freedom.

People naturally gravitate to one or two areas; that’s part of the journey. What matters is choosing a starting point and beginning to build.

Desde ese día empezamos un camino juntos, haciendo todo lo suficientemente sencillo para que un niño de 11 años lo entendiera y aprendiera paso a paso, ganando confianza.

Comenzó de inmediato. Con su mesada empezó comprando plata; luego pasó al oro. Conservar activos en lugar de gastarlos en juegos o dulces le enseñó disciplina, paciencia y el valor de construir. Cuando su presupuesto lo permitió, agregamos acciones—participación en la propiedad con paciencia e investigación. A la par, fue dando forma a una empresa de comida para perros. Y aunque todavía no ha comprado propiedades, sigue estudiando bienes raíces para estar listo cuando llegue el momento.

  • Blanco — Activos en Papel: acciones, opciones y fondos explicados en términos sencillos.
  • Azul — Negocios: propiedad, sistemas y escala para crear libertad.
