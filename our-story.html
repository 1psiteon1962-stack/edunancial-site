Our Story

One afternoon when my son was 11, he came into my room just as an electronic deposit notification arrived. His eyes went wide. “How did you make money like that?” I said, “By using my head.” Then I asked, “Do you want to work with your head?” He said, “Yes… but I don’t know how.” That’s when Edunancial was born.

At 11, he started buying silver coins—discipline and assets instead of candy and games. Around 12, he began analyzing stocks: overvalued or undervalued, and why. After he presented his reasons, we bought. His results averaged 7–12%. At 15, he added gold—and today we also consider gems like diamonds, rubies, or emeralds.

The Red, White, and Blue method doesn’t tell you what to buy—it teaches you to ask why you’re buying and what it will do for you, putting you in control.
