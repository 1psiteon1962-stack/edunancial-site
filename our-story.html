Our Story

One afternoon when my son was 11, I had just ended a call with a business client when my phone lit up with an online deposit. His eyes got wide and he asked, “How did you make money like that?”

“By using my head,” I told him. He took a breath and said, “I want to use my head too— but I don’t know how.”

That sentence changed everything. Right there at our kitchen table, Edunancial began. We made a simple plan: learn how money works—clearly, slowly, and practically—so he could use his head with confidence.

Learning to Use His Head

  • Foundations: budgeting with purpose, paying himself first, building an emergency cushion.
  • Real-world practice: small, steady actions—comparing prices, reading receipts, setting savings targets.
  • Thinking time: better questions—What’s the plan? What are the risks? What’s Plan B?

We kept it simple and hands-on. No jargon. One page at a time.

From 11 to 15

By 15, he had solid habits and real curiosity. He started buying small amounts of physical silver and gold, learned to read a company’s story beyond the ticker, and practiced how to select, buy, and sell stocks with patience—aiming for a realistic 8–12% ROI over time.

It wasn’t luck. It was using his head: clear thinking, risk management, respect for the long game.
