Our Story

One afternoon when my son was 11, I had just ended a call with a business client when my phone lit up with an online deposit. He looked at me with wide eyes and asked, “How did you make money like that?”

“By using my head,” I told him. He paused, thought about it, and said, “I want to use my head too—but I don’t know how.”

That sentence changed everything. Right there, at our kitchen table, Edunancial began. We made a decision: he would learn how money works—clearly, slowly, and practically—so he could use his head with confidence.

Learning to Use His Head

  • Foundations: budgeting with purpose, paying himself first, and building an emergency cushion.
  • Real-world practice: small, consistent actions—comparing prices, reading receipts, setting savings targets.
  • Thinking time: asking better questions: What’s the plan? What are the risks? What’s Plan B?

We kept it simple and hands-on. No jargon. One page at a time.

From 11 to 15

By 15, he had developed steady habits and a curious mind. He started buying small amounts of physical silver and gold, learned to read a company’s story beyond the ticker, and practiced how to select, buy, and sell stocks with patience—aiming for a consistent, realistic 8–12% ROI over time.

It wasn’t about luck. It was about using his head: thinking clearly, managing risk, and respecting the long game.
